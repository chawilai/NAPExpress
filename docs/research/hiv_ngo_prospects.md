# HIV NGO Prospects — NHSO Section 3 (มาตรา 3) HIV/AIDS Service Providers

**Research date:** 2026-04-13
**Target list:** 71 Thai NGO organizations registered as "หน่วยบริการรับส่งต่อเฉพาะด้าน HIV/AIDS" under NHSO Section 3
**Data compiled in:** `hiv_ngo_prospects.csv`

---

## 1. Summary

- **Organizations captured:** ~31 entries (some are branches of parent orgs)
- **Unique parent organizations:** ~18
- **Estimated coverage vs. 71-list:** ~25-40% (counting RSAT's 6 clinics + MPlus's 3 branches + SWING's 3 branches as distinct units)
- **Source reality:** The official complete list of 71 registered organizations is **not publicly posted** as a consolidated directory. NHSO announces registrations piecemeal via nhso.go.th news and board meeting press releases. To get the authoritative 71-list, contact:
  - NHSO hotline **1330 ext 5** (ฝ่ายสิทธิประโยชน์ / ฝ่ายสนับสนุนเครือข่าย)
  - **Thai NGO Coalition on AIDS (TNCA)** — 02-377-5255 (umbrella body, likely has the most current member roster)
  - **IHRI** — technical partner that runs the NHSO ม.3 registration / certification pipeline for community-led services
  - **Division of AIDS, TB and STIs, DDC (กรมควบคุมโรค)** — runs the community service quality assessment

## 3. Top 10 Priority Prospects for AutoNAP

Ranked by likely fit (volume, multi-site, tech-savvy, KP-led service delivery):

1. **Caremat Foundation (Chiang Mai)** — EXISTING CAREMAT USER. AutoNAP was purpose-built to integrate their workflow. Almost guaranteed first customer.
2. **MPlus Foundation (Chiang Mai + Chiang Rai + Phitsanulok)** — IHRI consortium partner; national KP-led backbone CBO; high volume. Single decision-maker unlocks 3 sites.
3. **RSAT (Bangkok HQ + 6 clinics nationwide)** — 6 clinics + 10 DICs; national coverage. HQ deal unlocks all branches; advocacy arm.
4. **SWING Foundation (Silom + Saphan Kwai + Pattaya)** — 20,000-30,000 clients/year across 3 clinics; FSW/MSW/TGSW focus; very high volume.
5. **Love Foundation (Chiang Mai)** — Digital-first, built Sisterhood app with Gilead support; tech-savvy, likely receptive to SaaS.
6. **Sisters Foundation (Pattaya)** — First TGW-led foundation; Princess PrEP participant; est. 2004.
7. **ACTTEAM Khon Kaen** — Registered Medical Technology Clinic; Global Fund/STAR funded; professional operation; Northeast anchor.
8. **M-Friends Udonthani Foundation** — LINKAGES-supported, 20-year operation; only major CBO in upper Northeast besides ACTTEAM.
9. **HON Foundation (Bangkok + Pattaya)** — Explicitly self-reported as NHSO ม.3 unit on their website; recent Bangkok expansion 2025 means they're actively growing.
10. **Ozone Foundation (Bangkok)** — Only major PWID/harm reduction CBO; unique niche; partner with Dreamlopments.

### Bonus: strategic non-customers worth contacting
- **TNCA (Thai NGO Coalition on AIDS)** — Not a customer but THE gatekeeper for introductions to all 71 orgs.
- **IHRI** — Runs the ม.3 certification pipeline; endorsement here = credibility with every CBO.

## 4. Gaps — What Wasn't Findable

- **The official 71-org consolidated roster** is not public. Matichon article (news_4720827) was inaccessible (403). NHSO news page (news/3832) returned 404. No single downloadable PDF found.
- **Small regional CBOs** in Deep South, Phuket, Surat Thani, Nong Khai are likely registered but not discoverable via Google/English search.
- **Contact persons / directors** — very few names captured publicly. Cold outreach requires a referral or calling the main line.
- **Service volume data** — no public numbers. SWING's 20-30k/yr is the only concrete figure found.
- **Email addresses** — most orgs only publish Facebook/LINE. Only ~6 confirmed official emails.
